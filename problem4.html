Friction is the force that opposes the motion of objects sliding or moving past each other. It occurs when two surfaces come into contact, and the microscopic bumps and irregularities on each surface catch and resist movement. Friction is essential in everyday life because it allows us to walk without slipping and cars to grip the road. However, it can also cause wear and tear on mechanical parts and reduce efficiency by turning some energy into heat.
